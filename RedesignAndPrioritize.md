# Things I would collect for EVERY property

This is the master list I'd build. Think of it as the blueprint for a truly comprehensive property intelligence platform.

### Identity & Trust

* Project name, builder, RERA details, launch year, possession date, approvals, escrow status, legal clearances.
* Builder profile: years in business, delivered projects, under-construction projects, average delay, customer ratings, awards, ISO certifications.

### Pricing & Financials

* Starting price, configuration-wise pricing, historical price changes, price per sq ft, negotiation range, booking amount, payment plans (CLP, Flexi, Down Payment, Subvention), PLC, maintenance charges, registration, GST, parking costs, clubhouse charges, total acquisition cost, home loan eligibility and partner banks.

### Investment Intelligence

* Expected appreciation, rental yield, resale demand, liquidity score, price benchmark against nearby projects, supply vs demand, infrastructure pipeline, appreciation catalysts, investment horizon recommendations.

### Project Quality

* Construction technology, density, open space percentage, tower count, unit count, floor count, green certifications, smart-home features, materials and brands used, power backup, water supply, parking ratio, EV charging, security systems.

### Lifestyle

* Amenities by category, clubhouse, sports, wellness, kids, senior citizens, pet friendliness, work-from-home spaces, community events.

### Location

* Exact geo coordinates, drive times, metro, highways, airports, schools, hospitals, malls, restaurants, offices, walkability, pollution, noise, green cover, crime perception, flood risk.

### Market Intelligence

* Competing projects, occupancy rates, inventory levels, absorption, upcoming launches, infrastructure projects, future supply.

### Buyer Experience

* Site visit availability, sample flat status, construction progress photos, drone videos, virtual tours, resident reviews, FAQs, downloadable brochures and layouts.

# My Biggest Recommendation

If I were leading this product, I would stop thinking in terms of **tabs** and start thinking in terms of **questions**.

Instead of asking, "What goes into the Pricing tab?", ask:

* "What would a buyer need to confidently say yes or no after reading this section?"
* "What would a builder most want to showcase to persuade that buyer?"

Every screen should move the user one step closer to a confident decision.

## Where I think we should go next

I would **not** jump into redesigning random screens.

Instead, I'd take a more strategic approach:

1. **Redesign the first Overview screen from scratch** using everything we've learned.
2. Establish a **design language and information hierarchy** that all other tabs inherit.
3. Define **importance tiers** (critical, supporting, optional) so every card knows where it belongs.
4. Build a consistent pattern for AI insights, trust indicators, financial intelligence, and builder marketing across every tab.
5. Only then redesign Analysis, Residences, Pricing, Location, and Documents using the same system.

That way the entire experience feels like one cohesive product instead of six independently designed pages.

I genuinely think RealtyPals has the potential to become something that doesn't just help users *find* properties—it helps them *choose* the right one. That's a much stronger position in the market, and your current data model is already rich enough to support it. The next step is making the UI tell that story.
